v6 — what changed from v5, and why
1
The daily schedule was dishonest. v5's own skeleton (9AM–8PM with breaks) only supports 9.0h of real study time, but 24 of 31 weeks claimed 9.5h or 10h/day — numbers the schedule itself couldn't deliver. Fixed by defining two explicit, named skeletons below: a Standard day (9.0h, used for most weeks) and an Intensive day (9.5h, an honestly wider day used only in weeks tagged "intensive" — hard chapters or test-adjacent weeks). No week in v6 exceeds 9.5h, and every week's hours are now re-verified to sum correctly against its own subject breakdown.
2
Kinematics 1D and 2D are no longer merged. v4/v5 combined both into one week and called them "routine for droppers" — an assumption that doesn't hold, since gaps in exactly this kind of numerically-dense problem-solving are often why a student is repeating the year. Each now gets its own full week. The lost week was recovered without touching any other chapter: the old separate "self-test/diagnostic" week was folded into Day 1 of the merged revision+mock week instead of staying as its own week — both still happen, just sequenced together, since both were already content-free weeks.
3
Semi Major Test 2 had zero buffer before it — every other Allen test had a full week. RT1, SM1, and RT2 were each preceded by a dedicated no-new-content buffer week. SM2 wasn't: new content finished Friday, the test was Sunday. Fixed by moving Error Analysis and EM Waves (both genuinely trivial, <1h chapters) one week earlier, which lets the SM2-adjacent week finish all new content by Thursday — leaving Friday and Saturday as a real mock/consolidation buffer, matching the pattern used everywhere else.
4
A promised rest day never actually existed. v5 said once, in one week's note, that "Sunday rest moves to Thursday afternoon" — and then never scheduled it, for 11 straight weeks. Fixed by building a real half-day into the weekly rhythm everywhere mocks run on Sunday: Saturday afternoon becomes genuinely light (no new content, optional revision only), every week, shown explicitly in that week's own schedule rather than mentioned once and dropped.
5
The plan ended in January but BITSAT is in April — a 10-week gap with no plan at all. Real BITSAT prep hours were carved out of nearly every week from October onward, but the plan stopped the moment JEE Main Session 1 ended, leaving zero guidance for the 10 weeks before the actual BITSAT exam. Added Phase 5 (Wks 32–36, Feb–Apr): a dedicated BITSAT-intensive runway covering the syllabus topics JEE doesn't (English Proficiency, Logical Reasoning depth, BITSAT-specific Physics/Chem/Maths question style) plus a proper mock-test taper into the exam.
6
One content-dense week had half the study time of every other week. The week covering SHM, Fluid Mechanics, Semiconductor, and five organic chapters was reframed in an earlier patch as "weekly totals" to fix an apparent overrun — but the new weekly total (30h) was actually less than half of a normal week's capacity (~57h), even though this was meant to be the densest week in the plan. Recomputed properly: with Error Analysis and EM Waves moved out (see #3), the real content totals ~25.5h, which fits comfortably across a 6-day week at the standard pace — with two genuine buffer days left over before SM2.
